## Operating philosophy (derived from how this workspace actually runs)

These are not aspirations — they are patterns already enforced in this workspace's rules,
named here so content can express them:

1. **Canon lives in one place; everything else is a projection.** The folder on disk is
   the truth; dashboards, wikis, briefs, and decks are views of it. When a view disagrees
   with the source, fix the view. EverPass thinking works the same way in deals: get the
   underlying facts right and every downstream conversation gets easier.

2. **The human decides; the system prepares.** Automation drafts, syncs, flags, and
   assembles — but a person sends the email, approves the recategorization, signs off the
   HIGH-impact change. Speed comes from preparation, not from removing judgment.

3. **Freshness is a feature.** Nothing user-facing may silently go stale (12-hour rule,
   enforced). A number without a timestamp is a liability; being visibly current is part
   of being trustworthy.

4. **Manual override always wins.** A live decision by the operator beats any scheduled
   process. Systems that fight their operators get abandoned.

5. **Compounding beats heroics.** Lessons get logged, reviewed, and folded back into the
   rules (see the self-improvement loop). The workspace gets smarter every session instead
   of re-learning the same corrections.
