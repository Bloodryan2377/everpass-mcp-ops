# Leveling and Comp Audit: Content Partnerships Hires

**Prepared:** 2026-07-09
**Scope:** The two new JDs (Director, Content Partnerships and Manager, Content Partnerships) benchmarked against EverPass's live media and content roles.
**Source of the ask:** *EverPass Media, 2026 YTD, The Case for the Team* (board / CFO funding case). Three seats requested: one Director/VP and two Managers, the two Managers combined here into one JD and one talent pool.

## Recommendation up front

1. **Director at $185,000 base is in band.** It sits inside EverPass's existing Director of Content & Business Development range ($150,000 to $225,000). Keep the Director title; hold VP for a later leveling conversation.
2. **Manager at $135,000 base is over band.** EverPass's current Manager of Content & Business Development band tops out at $120,000. Paying a "Manager" $135,000 breaks internal equity by $15,000 over band. Re-title to **Senior Manager, Content Partnerships** at $135,000, or hold the Manager title and align base to $120,000.
3. **The combined Manager JD is viable as requested,** but the two seats cover different skill axes (technical/product versus strategy/comms). Post one JD, screen the two hires toward complementary strengths.

## Finding 1: Director, $185,000 base plus 20% bonus

- **In band.** $185,000 sits at roughly the 55th percentile of the existing Director of Content & Business Development range ($150,000 to $225,000). Consistent and defensible.
- **Title.** Use "Director, Content Partnerships" to match the "Director, [Function]" convention already in use (Director, National Accounts). The funding case says "Director/VP." At $185,000 base, the number aligns to Director, not VP, in EverPass's current structure. If the role formally carries two reports plus independent deal authority and EverPass wants to signal seniority to counterparties, VP framing is supportable, but that is a leveling decision, not a comp-band one. Recommendation: Director now, revisit VP after the team is stood up.
- **Bonus.** 20% target is consistent with director-tier variable at EverPass. Loaded cost of approximately $255,000 (base, bonus and benefits) is a budget figure and stays out of the candidate-facing JD.

## Finding 2: Manager, $135,000 base plus 10% bonus

- **Over band.** EverPass's current Manager of Content & Business Development band is $90,000 to $120,000. $135,000 is $15,000 above the top of that band.
- **Why the scope justifies a premium.** The new Manager owns content API and backend scheduler work and the engineering-to-strategy bridge. That is heavier and more technical than the standard content BD Manager, whose scope is sourcing, research and deal support. The premium is earned by scope; the problem is only the label.
- **Two clean options:**
  - **(a) Re-title to Senior Manager, Content Partnerships at $135,000.** EverPass already runs a Senior Manager tier (Sr. Manager, Data, Analytics & AI), so this is consistent and protects internal equity. Recommended.
  - **(b) Hold the Manager title and set base at $120,000** (top of the existing band). Preserves the title but underpays the scope and risks losing candidates who can do both the technical and strategic halves.
- **Recommendation: option (a).** The scope maps to Senior Manager, and titling it Senior Manager avoids paying a Manager $15,000 over band, which would compress against every existing Manager.

## Finding 3: The combined JD and one talent pool

- The funding case describes two distinct seats: Operations / PM (API, scheduler, engineering bridge, workstream ownership) and Strategy / Comms (funnel, GTM, analytics, cross-functional communication). The request is to combine them into one JD and one talent pool.
- **Viable, with one caveat.** The combined profile is a technical-commercial operator. Candidates genuinely strong on both axes are rarer, so expect either a longer search or a decision to weight each of the two hires toward one axis.
- **Recommendation:** post the single combined JD (done), but in screening, deliberately balance the two hires: one weighted toward content operations and product, one toward strategy and communication, so the pair covers both axes while sharing the same core profile. The JD is written to support this.

## Finding 4: Experience bands and structure

- Director at 8+ years and Manager at 4 to 7 years match EverPass norms (Manager of Content & Business Development is posted at 4 to 7 years). Consistent.
- Bonus structure (20% Director, 10% Manager) aligns with EverPass leveling, where director-tier carries higher variable. Consistent.
- Location and benefits: EverPass content roles are New York, NY hybrid with the standard benefits stack. Both JDs carry the standard benefits and equal-opportunity language for consistency.

## Net

- Director: **approve as written**, Director title, $185,000 base plus 20%, in band.
- Manager: **approve the scope and the $135,000 number**, but level it as **Senior Manager** to keep it in band and protect internal equity. If the Manager title is required, set base at $120,000.
- Combined JD: **approve**, screen the two hires for complementary strengths.
