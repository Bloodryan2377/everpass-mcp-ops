# AUDIENCE.md — Who EverPass Content Serves

Layer-1 core instruction (tool-agnostic). Read before creating any artifact with a reader
other than the current session — decks, briefs, partner drafts, dashboard narrative.
Three distinct tiers exist in this workspace; naming the tier first is half the writing.

---

## Tier 1 — Internal operations (Ryan as operator; future operators; AI sessions)

- **Daily reality:** running 35+ partner relationships through a cockpit of dashboards,
  briefs, insights files, and scheduled automations — while also being the CCO whose real
  job is the deals themselves. Context-switching is the tax; anything that adds a read
  without adding a decision is a cost.
- **What they need from an artifact:** the state, the delta since last time, and the next
  action — in that order, in seconds. `Rule / Why / How` for anything meant to persist.
- **Language they use:** partner slugs, entry types (`MEETING | EMAIL | DOC | DEAL |
  NOTE | DECISION | RISK`), skill names, "canonical", "projection", "freshness",
  "source of truth".
- **Trust erodes when:** data is stale without being marked stale; automation overwrote a
  manual edit; a report says "done" and `git log` disagrees.

## Tier 2 — Internal mixed-expertise (leadership, sales, ops, new hires in live meetings)

- **Daily reality:** in the room for the decision, not the plumbing. They see the deck
  once, live, possibly over video, and will not read it again.
- **What they need:** a narrative with one idea per slide, a takeaway headline they could
  repeat afterward, and the recommendation stated — not implied.
- **Language they use:** deal stage, launch date, go/no-go, economics, pipeline.
- **Trust erodes when:** slides are dense, jargon assumes context they lack, or the deck
  looks like generic AI output instead of a considered EverPass artifact.

## Tier 3 — External partners (Disney, ESPN, NBCU, Netflix, Charter, leagues, RSNs, distributors)

- **Daily reality:** their exec/legal/content teams juggle many counterparties; EverPass
  gets minutes of attention. Everything shared externally may be forwarded beyond the
  intended reader.
- **What they need:** brand-level professionalism, positions stated cleanly with support
  behind them, numbers that carry dates, zero surprises about who is named. Published
  surfaces go through Netlify, not raw file shares.
- **Language they use:** rights, territories, term, exclusivity, carve-outs, renewals,
  launch windows, promotion, distribution footprint.
- **Trust erodes when:** individuals are named in shared material; a stale figure is
  presented as current; internal shorthand leaks into external framing.
